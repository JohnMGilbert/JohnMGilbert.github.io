Long before software, I learned patience and process in the kitchen. Baking still influences the way I think about iteration, timing, and craft.

Don't buy bread

Okay, maybe that's a little aggressive. Sourdough takes time, attention, and a starter you treat like a tiny living roommate. But fresh bread you made yourself still feels hard to beat.

Basic loaf recipe

Here's the formula behind the loaf above.

Ingredients

  • 450g bread flour
  • 50g whole wheat flour
  • 360g water, divided
  • 90g ripe sourdough starter
  • 10g salt

Instructions

  1. Mix the flour with 350g of water until just combined, then rest for 1 hour.
  2. Add the sourdough starter and salt, then mix until fully incorporated.
  3. Bulk ferment at room temperature, performing 3 sets of stretch-and-folds over the first 90 minutes.
  4. Pre-shape, rest 20 to 30 minutes, then shape into a tight round or oval.
  5. Proof at room temperature for 1 to 2 hours or refrigerate overnight.
  6. Bake in a preheated Dutch oven at 450F for 20 minutes covered, then 25 to 30 minutes uncovered.
  7. Cool completely before slicing.
